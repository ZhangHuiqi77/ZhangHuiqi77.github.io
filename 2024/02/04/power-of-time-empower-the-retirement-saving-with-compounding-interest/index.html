power of time - empower the retirement saving with compounding interest

Posted on 2024-02-04

We always hear: keep doing and you will get a lot. This is actually describe the benefit from compounding interest, in other words, power of time. But it is not intuitive how much I can get. If not clear enough, our brain will not set the specific goal and really make it. To specify this effect, let’s apply it in calculating the retirement saving. If you can put the specific amount of money (here 50k) every year with specific compounding interest (here 3% per year) until you are 55 years old (here start saving at 2 years old). Let’s see.

First, creat a class called power_of_time, it can also be used to minic compounding interet effect of doing exercise, writing, reading, etc…

Then finish the script, so we can use power_of_time.accum() method to mimic once accumulate:

The plot is like this:
https://github.com/ZhangHuiqi77/docs/blob/main/empower-the-retirement-saving-with-compounding-interest.jpg

So if I save 50k per year from I am 27 with 3% interest rate, I will have 2328800 when I am 55, 1.606 times of initial saving money!
I think precisely know the benefit will make people feel more safety when they save and spend money. And every time when saving, the reward of doing so is amplified, because oringinally it could happen after like 30 years! This positive feedback make people easier to persist and enjoy the moment.

I want to make the ‘power_of_time’ class be applied more in my life. In addition, about object-oriented programming, it is better for an object, which have many properties and also some specific methods. ‘Class’ is easy to expand and also do team coding. Usually use ‘handle’ when define it, so the changing of properties will be assigned to the initial. Properties, as the name, show the properties of the object. And methods could be many functions that can be used to the object. Usually the first function is to generate an example of the class, obj, and meanwhile initialize the properties.
